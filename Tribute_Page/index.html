Billy Graham

"My home is in Heaven. I'm just traveling through this world."

Here's a time line of Bill Graham's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Charlotte, North Carolina to William Franklin Billy Graham Senior, a businessman and farmer and his wife Morrow Coffee Graham. Eldest of the family with two younger sisters and brother.
  • 1934 - At the age of 16, he attended a series of revival meetings in Charlotte by an evangelist, Mordecai Fowler Ham, which changed his personality and he decided to commit his life to Christ.
  • 1936 - Graduated from Sharon High School in 1936 and enrolled at Bob Jones College, Cleveland but left it after a few months. In 1937, he attended the Florida Bible Institute and graduated with a bachelor degree in theology in 1940.
  • 1938 - He was baptized by the church's pastor, Rev. Cecil Underwood in Silver Lake. Later, he was ordained by Rev. Underwood and other local pastors as a Southern Baptist minister in the St. John's River Association.
  • 1940 - He enrolled at the Wheaton College, Illinois, and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in anthropology in 1943.
  • 1949 - A group called ‘Christ for Greater Los Angeles’ invited him to preach at their L.A. revival and people emerged in large numbers to witness this event. This extended the revival for an additional five weeks and with the heavy coverage from the wire services and newspapers, he became a national figure.
  • 1950 - He founded the ‘Billy Graham Evangelistic Association’ ministry along with his colleagues to expand their community.
  • 1964 - He contributed significantly to the Civil Rights Movement and became one of the closest friends of Rev. Martin Luther King, Jr. He also held integrated crusades in Birmingham, Alabama, on Easter 1964 in the aftermath of the bombing of the Sixteenth Street Baptist Church.
  • 1989 - Graham received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 1991 - Billy Graham held his largest event in North America on the Great Lawn of New York's Central Park.
  • 2000 - He received the Ronald Reagan Presidential Foundation Freedom Award, for monumental and lasting contributions to the cause of freedom in 2000.
  • 2001 - He was awarded the Honorary Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (KBE) for his international contribution to civic and religious life for over 60 years.
  • 1943 - Met and married Ruth McCue Bell, daughter of a surgeon, while studying at Wheaton College, after his graduation in anthropology. They had 5 Children.
  • 2018 - Billy Graham dies at the age of 100.
